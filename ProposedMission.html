Proposed Space Mission

Theoretical mission to deploy a constellation of GPS satellites in Mars orbit.

I undertook the challenge of conceptualizing a space mission aimed at launching four satellites into Mars orbit to establish a GPS navigation constellation. These satellites would play a crucial role in facilitating future manned missions and rover explorations on the Martian surface by providing real-time navigation data. Drawing inspiration from existing space missions such as the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO) and MAVEN, as well as GPS III and IIIF satellites, I meticulously designed the mission architecture and spacecraft systems to ensure the successful deployment and operation of the satellite constellation.

My responsibilities encompassed a wide range of tasks, from conducting heritage research on previous space missions to estimating mass budgets for the spacecraft and propulsion systems. Leveraging the principles of systems engineering, I meticulously calculated the mass, cost, and power estimates for various components, including propulsion systems, communications systems, power systems, and structural elements. Through this process, I gained valuable insights into the intricate planning and design considerations involved in space missions, learning to balance technical requirements, performance objectives, and resource constraints to achieve mission success.

Furthermore, this project provided me with a hands-on experience in applying theoretical knowledge to practical scenarios, honing my skills in problem-solving, critical thinking, and interdisciplinary collaboration. By navigating through the complexities of mission planning, I developed a deeper appreciation for the multifaceted nature of space exploration and the importance of meticulous planning and analysis in ensuring the success of such endeavors.
